About Mindful Living Today

Welcome to Mindful Living Today, a platform dedicated to exploring the art of slow living. Our mission is to help people find more joy, fulfillment, and meaning in their lives by embracing a slower, more intentional way of living.
At Mindful Living Today, we believe that the modern world is often too fast-paced and frenzied, leaving little time for reflection, connection, and creativity. That's why we're committed to promoting a more mindful, purposeful way of living that emphasizes quality over quantity, presence over productivity, and simplicity over excess.

Our team consists of a group of passionate writers, artists, and creatives who share a common love of slow living. Through our blog, resources, and products, we aim to provide inspiration, guidance, and tools for anyone who wants to slow down and savor the richness of life.

Join us on our journey towards a slower, more meaningful existence and discover the beauty of the art of slow living.

The Art of Slow Living

The slow living movement emerged in the late 1980s as a response to the fast-paced and consumer-driven culture of the modern world. The movement's origins can be traced back to Italy, where a group of activists, artists, and intellectuals founded the "Slow Food" movement in protest against the rise of fast food and the globalization of culinary culture.

Slow Food aimed to promote traditional and local food cultures, as well as sustainable agriculture and responsible consumption. The movement quickly gained popularity and spread beyond Italy, inspiring a broader movement for slow living that encompassed not just food, but all aspects of daily life.


In the years since its inception, the slow living movement has continued to evolve and grow, with many influential figures and ideas contributing to its development. Some key figures in the slow living movement include Carl Honoré, the author of "In Praise of Slow," and Brooke McAlary, the founder of the Slow Home podcast and blog.

Today, the slow living movement continues to inspire people around the world to live more intentionally and deliberately, to reconnect with their communities and the natural world, and to find greater fulfillment and meaning in their lives.

Our Core Principles

The art of slow living is rooted in a set of core principles that prioritize quality over quantity, mindfulness over distraction, and sustainability over excess. These principles include:

Mindfulness: The practice of being present and aware of one's thoughts, feelings, and surroundings. Mindfulness is an essential component of slow living because it helps us to slow down and savor the richness of life, rather than rushing through it mindlessly.

Simplicity: The idea of living with less and focusing on what truly matters. Simplicity is about reducing the noise and clutter in our lives, so we can better appreciate the things that bring us joy and fulfillment.

Sustainability: The concept of living in harmony with the environment and preserving resources for future generations. Sustainability involves making conscious choices about how we consume, produce, and dispose of goods and services, to minimize our impact on the planet.

Connection: The importance of building meaningful relationships with others and fostering a sense of community. Connection is about recognizing our interdependence with others and finding ways to collaborate and support one another.

These core principles are interconnected, and each one reinforces the others. For example, by practicing mindfulness, we can cultivate a greater appreciation for simplicity and sustainability, and by focusing on sustainability, we can build stronger connections with others who share our values. By embracing these principles, we can create a more meaningful and fulfilling way of living that benefits both ourselves and the world around us.
